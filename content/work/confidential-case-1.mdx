<Chapter title="Context">

<ConfidentialBlock>

This engagement involved a mid-market SaaS company operating in the enterprise resource planning sector. The platform had grown organically over eight years, accumulating significant interaction debt — inconsistent navigation patterns, redundant workflows, and a component library split across three generations of design tooling.

The engagement spanned six months and covered discovery, information architecture, visual design, and frontend implementation of the new design system.

</ConfidentialBlock>

</Chapter>

<Chapter title="Problem">

<ConfidentialBlock>

The core issue was not aesthetic — it was cognitive load. Power users were navigating an average of 14 screens to complete a task that should require 3. New users spent their first two weeks just learning where things lived, not learning how to do their actual jobs.

Exit surveys cited "confusing navigation" as the top reason for churn in the 90-day window.

**Key metrics before redesign:**
- Average task completion: 6.2 minutes
- Onboarding time to first value: 3 business days
- Support ticket rate (navigation-related): 34% of all tickets
- NPS: 22

</ConfidentialBlock>

</Chapter>

<Chapter title="Approach">

<ConfidentialBlock>

The project began with a two-week discovery sprint. We conducted 18 contextual interviews with users across three role types: administrators, power users, and occasional contributors. We mapped every workflow end-to-end and tagged each step with friction indicators.

Three structural insights emerged:

1. **Role-based context collapse** — the platform showed the same navigation to all roles despite wildly different job functions. Admins and contributors were sharing the same sidebar.
2. **Action-first vs. object-first mismatch** — the platform was organized around object types (invoices, contracts, vendors) but users thought in terms of actions (approve, review, send).
3. **Modal debt** — 60% of data entry happened inside stacked modals, which created a disorienting back-stack and broke the browser's native navigation model.

The redesign addressed each systematically.

</ConfidentialBlock>

</Chapter>

<Chapter title="Outcome">

<ConfidentialBlock>

After a phased rollout over 10 weeks, the new platform showed measurable improvement across all tracked metrics:

- **Average task completion:** 6.2 min → 2.1 min (−66%)
- **Onboarding time to first value:** 3 days → 4 hours (−87%)
- **Navigation-related support tickets:** 34% → 9% of total
- **NPS:** 22 → 51

The design system — 84 components with documented variants, motion tokens, and accessibility audits — was adopted as the company's single source of truth across web and mobile.

</ConfidentialBlock>

</Chapter>
